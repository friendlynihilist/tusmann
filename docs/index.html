Who is Tusmann?

Nel racconto “La scelta della fidanzata” (Die Brautwahl) il grande scrittore tedesco E.T.A. Hoffmann narra le singolari avventure dei tre pretendenti alla mano di una giovane e affascinante fanciulla. Uno solo di loro avrà la mano della ragazza, ma gli altri due riceveranno, in cambio della rinuncia, dei doni per loro ancor più preziosi. In particolare il cancelliere Tusmann, curiosa figura di attempato bibliofilo che difficilmente avrebbe potuto rendere davvero felice la giovane, riceve un dono che non potrebbe lasciarlo più soddisfatto: un libro magico, dalle pagine bianche ma capace di trasformarsi all’istante in qualunque altro libro al semplice desiderio del suo proprietario. Il libro magico del cancelliere Tusmann è una sorta di libro universale, e le sue pagine bianche possono riempirsi, a comando, con i testi più diversi: il sogno di ogni bibliofilo. Anche lo schermo di un computer può riempirsi con i testi più diversi. In un certo senso, il computer assomiglia molto al libro magico del cancelliere Tusmann.

After working as educator, Aldus Manutius (1449/1452 – 6 February 1515) decided to devote the later part of his life to publishing and disseminating rare texts (especially Greek and Latin classics). Many intellectuals like the philosopher Erasmus highly esteemed Manutius for his tireless efforts in restoring ancient learning. In addition to their textual value, many Aldine editions embody Aldus restless efforts to re-configure the printed book both as a typographical and commercial product, marking him as an innovative publisher of his age. His enchiridia, small portable books, revolutionized personal reading and are the predecessor of the modern paperback.

Printer's mark

The symbol of the dolphin and anchor has been used since Roman times to illustrate the adage Festina Lente. This emblem appeared for the first time as Manutius' mark in 1502, and in contrast to the tradition before Aldus, it was located in the first pages of the book instead of the ending pages.

Margins

In ancient paginated books, margin measures were chosen accordingly to the reconstructed Van de Graaf's canon and resulted specularly symmetrical. This "secret canon" allowed for the construction of a pleasingly divided page with proportions 2:3:4:6 (inner:top:outer:bottom). I checked proportions from several Aldine Editions images on the web and found the correct ratio between margins, images and font size.

Typeface

Manutius commissioned the punchcutter Francesco Griffo of Bologna to create a new typeface designed to look like the handwriting of humanists both in Latin and Greek, since at the time printed works only imitated formal manuscript hands (either blackletter or the humanistic littera antica). Manutius and Griffo's original typeface is the first known model of italic type. I chose a font ("Griffo Classico") that had the most similar characteristics: letters appear in high contrast with serif. For frontispiece and image captions I used a small capital font ("Griffo Classico Small Caps").

Initials

I decided to imitate the elegant initials with branch, tendril and grapevine decorations (known as bianchi girari – white vine stem decoration) with a delicate and simple font called "Roycroft".

Images

It turned out to be impossible to reproduce the extraordinary skill of the craftsmen who engraved the sophisticated illustrations of many Aldine editions, expression of the “classical” style generally regarded as typical of the best Venetian illustrations of the late 1490s. I decided to emulate the style of the image borders and the high-contrast greyscale of the images. It will still be possible to see the original colors by hovering on the image.

Although painting was its most conspicuous medium, Rimpa was about design, total effect, taste that embraced calligraphy, textiles, ceramics and lacquer. It embraces art marked by a bold, graphic abbreviation of natural motifs, the lavish use of expensive mineral and metallic pigments, incorporation of calligraphy into painting compositions, and innovative experimentation with new brush techniques.
